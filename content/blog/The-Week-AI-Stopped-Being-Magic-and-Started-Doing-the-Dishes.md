### The Apple in the Room

The biggest headline, of course, was Apple’s unveiling of "Apple Intelligence." While the name might lack a certain poetic flair, the philosophy behind it is profound. For years, the AI arms race has been about building the biggest, most powerful brain in a server farm somewhere in Oregon or Virginia. Apple’s approach is fundamentally different. It's about putting a smaller, but deeply personal, brain right into your pocket.

By focusing on on-device processing for most tasks, Apple is making a statement: AI shouldn't just be powerful; it should be *yours*. It should know that when you say "show me pictures of mom at the beach," it knows who your mom is and can find photos from your trip last July, all without sending your personal data on a round-trip to the cloud. It’s not about generating a photorealistic dragon; it’s about cleaning up your inbox, summarizing your meeting notes, and creating a fun little animation of your dad for his birthday.

This is the "doing the dishes" phase of AI. It's less glamorous than writing a novel, but for most people, it's infinitely more useful.
